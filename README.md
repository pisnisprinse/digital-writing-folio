### about me
what types of writing do you do (e.g. fiction, nonfiction, poetry)?
```
i write a lot of sci-fi/fantasy because i have a lot of ideas for sci-fi/fantasy. i enjoy writing memoir and poetry, but it's enjoyable because it's a challenge to overcome, rather than because it comes naturally to me. writing digitally is really really interesting to me, and i love the formatting possibilities it opens up; i especially love figuring out how i can tailor the form of a work to suit it's themes.
```
what tools (or techniques) do you use to write? why do you use them?
```
i do most of my writing on google docs, but i always keep a journal on me as well. my process involves writing down a lot of ideas as i have them, and then returning to them when i have the spare time to sit down and write, to see if any ideas jump out at me as particularly interesting or worth exploring. i have a separate google account just for my ongoing writing projects so i can keep my writing thoughts organised and quarantined from the rest of the pish.
```
how do these tools shape the way you write (e.g., constrain or generate)?
```
having a separate writing account helps me keep my thoughts focused and my ideas organised, which is very helpful as someone who'll return to ideas weeks or even months after leaving them. having a scratchpad for my broader ideas can also give me inspiration on combining them or integrating elements of one into another. because it's all linked to one google account, i'm also able to write whenever or wherever as long as i've got at least my phone on me. Of course, the downside of this is that having all my different projects stored in the same place and accessible whenever means that it's very easy for me to flit between projects without having to commit to one and actually finish.
```
what are you curious about writing in the digital environment?
```
pieces like *the gathering cloud* are very interesting to me: i'm very interested in using the digital environment as a tool to help me layer meaning on top of my works in interesting new ways.
```
